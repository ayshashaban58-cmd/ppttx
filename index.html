Power Factor Explained

Power Factor (PF) = Real Power (kW) / Apparent Power (kVA)

Think of it like beer: kW is the beer, kVA is the mug (beer + foam).

You're charged for the whole mug, but only the beer does work!

Low Power Factor = Big Problems

  • Utility Penalties: Extra charges below 0.9 PF
  • I²R Losses: Higher current → more heat in cables
  • Overloaded System: Transformers & breakers stressed
  • Voltage Drop: Poor motor performance, flickering

A lagging PF is a silent energy thief

Solution: Cancel Reactive Power

Most loads are inductive → absorb lagging kVAR

Add capacitors → supply leading kVAR

L + C = Neutralized Q → PF ≈ 1.0

Capacitors act as local kVAR generators

Method 1: Static PFC (Capacitor Banks)

Fixed capacitors switched in steps via contactors.

  • Low Cost & Proven Technology
  • Long Life (10–20 years)
  • No real-time adjustment
  • Risk of over-correction → leading PF

Method 2: Dynamic PFC (APF / STATCOM)

Active electronics inject exact kVAR in real-time.

  • PF > 0.99 achievable
  • Adapts to load changes instantly
  • Also fixes harmonics & flicker
  • High cost (10x static)

Why PFC Matters in Power Systems

  • Green: Reduces transmission losses
  • Saves Money: No penalties
  • Stable Grid: Frees capacity

Good PF = Efficient Machines = Reliable Power
